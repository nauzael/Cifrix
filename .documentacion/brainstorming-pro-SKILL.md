# Brainstorming Pro

Generate high-quality ideas with systematic evaluation, filtering, and a clear final recommendation.

## When to Use This Skill

Trigger this skill when users request:
- Creative ideas with a structured, curated approach
- Brainstorming sessions with selection criteria and recommendations
- Multiple options evaluated against specific requirements
- Problem-solving that requires both ideation and judgment
- Decisions between creative alternatives with clear reasoning

## Workflow

The skill follows a four-phase process:

### Phase 1: Clarification and Context Gathering

Before generating ideas, establish clear parameters:

1. **Understand the goal**: What specific problem or need are we solving?
2. **Define success criteria**: What makes an idea "good"? (e.g., feasibility, creativity, cost, impact, user appeal)
3. **Identify constraints**: Budget, timeline, audience, technical limitations, brand guidelines
4. **Determine output format**: How many ideas? How much detail per idea?

Ask clarifying questions only if essential information is missing. Avoid overwhelming the user with questions—combine related questions into 2-3 concise inquiries.

### Phase 2: Ideation

Generate a pool of diverse ideas by:

1. **Variety first**: Create ideas across a spectrum (conservative to radical, simple to complex, proven to experimental)
2. **Quantity over quality initially**: Generate 8-12 ideas to ensure diversity
3. **Document each idea briefly**: One-sentence summary + 2-3 key features or benefits

### Phase 3: Evaluation and Filtering

Apply systematic filtering:

1. **Create an evaluation matrix**: Use the success criteria from Phase 1
2. **Score or rank each idea**: Against 3-5 key criteria (e.g., feasibility: 1-5, creativity: 1-5, alignment: yes/no)
3. **Filter based on constraints**: Remove ideas that violate hard constraints (budget, timeline, brand fit)
4. **Narrow to top 3-5**: Keep the strongest contenders

### Phase 4: Final Recommendation and Detailed Analysis

For the top candidates:

1. **Provide detailed breakdown**: For each finalist, include:
   - Why it works (strengths)
   - Potential challenges (weaknesses)
   - Implementation considerations
   - Expected outcomes or success metrics

2. **Give a clear recommendation**: Based on the criteria and context, identify which idea(s) best solve the problem. Explain your reasoning concisely.

3. **Offer next steps**: What should the user do next? Prototype, validate, iterate, or decide between the top options?

## Output Structure

Format your response as follows:

```
## Ideas Generated
[List of 8-12 ideas with one-line descriptions]

## Evaluation & Filtering
[Criteria table or scoring summary showing how ideas performed]
[Filtered list: Top 3-5 ideas that made it through]

## Finalist Analysis
### [Top Idea 1]
- **Strengths**: [2-3 key advantages]
- **Challenges**: [Potential obstacles]
- **Implementation**: [How to make it real]
- **Potential Outcome**: [What success looks like]

### [Top Idea 2]
[Same structure]

## Recommendation
**Best Option: [Idea Name]**
[1-2 paragraphs explaining why this idea best meets your criteria and constraints]

## Next Steps
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]
- [Action 3]
```

## Key Principles

1. **Structure Builds Confidence**: The systematic approach ensures both ideation quality and decision-making rigor
2. **Criteria Matter Most**: Quality filtering depends on clear, user-defined success criteria
3. **Show Your Reasoning**: Users need to understand why certain ideas scored higher and why you recommend one
4. **Balance Creativity with Practicality**: Ideas should be imaginative but grounded in the user's reality
5. **One Clear Winner**: End with a single recommendation, not a tie between equals. Break ties by considering user priorities and constraints

## Tips for Excellence

- **Ask first, then ideate**: Never skip clarification. The best ideas come from truly understanding the problem
- **Diverse thinking**: Generate ideas from different angles (user perspective, technical perspective, market perspective, creative perspective)
- **Criteria transparency**: Make your evaluation criteria explicit and easy to follow
- **Confidence in recommendation**: Your recommendation should feel justified, not arbitrary. Explain the decision chain clearly
- **Actionable next steps**: Always end with clear, concrete next steps the user can take
